Does God know the future?

The obvious answer seems to be, “Yes,” especially given the prophetic predictions we read in Scripture. While I certainly grant that it’s possible, I think it is equally possible, and possibly even more likely, that He doesn’t, at least not in the way we generally mean when we talk about knowing the future.

God Himself pre-exists time, and, as its creator, is not bound by time. We read in 2 Peter 3:8, for example, that, "For the Lord, one day is like a thousand years, and a thousand years are like one day."

I have often heard it said that, because God exists outside of time, He can necessarily interact with all times simultaneously. I have heard examples given like watching a parade from the top of a skyscraper vs. watching from the street. The person on the street can only see the portion of the parade directly in front of them, but the person on the roof of the skyscraper can see the whole parade, beginning to end. The person on the skyscraper could tell the person on the street what will pass them next because they are able to see it all. With this understanding, God is able to tell the future in the Scriptures because He has already seen it happen and is dictating what He already knows is foreordained.

This assumes a lot about how God does/doesn’t interact with time. Isn’t it also possible that the future truly does not yet exist? Rather than watching a parade from a high vantage point, the example I would give is a Rube Goldberg machine. The creator sets up the machine and lets it run. At any given point, the machine will have reached a given point, and its future is what will happen if/when it continues, but it hasn’t happened yet. The creator is not part of the system, but they are also not barred from interfering/interacting with the system, but if they want to interact with it, they have to interact wherever it is in it’s execution. The past is truly past, and the future has truly not yet happened.
