[*Intro to the JBT*](https://www.thoughtsontheword.com/p/jbt-joes-bible-translation.html)

<sup>1</sup>Paul, a prisoner of Christ Jesus, and Timothy our brother, to Philemon our beloved friend and fellow worker, <sup>2</sup>to Apphia our sister, to Archippus our fellow soldier, and to the church in your house: <sup>3</sup>Grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

<sup>4</sup>I thank my God always, as I remember you in my prayers, <sup>5</sup>because I hear of your love and of the faith that you have toward the Lord Jesus and for all the saints: <sup>6</sup>I pray that the faith you share with us might deepen your understanding of every good thing you have in Christ. <sup>7</sup>For I have had much joy and encouragement because of your love, for the hearts of the saints have been refreshed through you, brother.

<sup>8</sup>So, although I have enough confidence in Christ to command you to do what is proper, <sup>9</sup>I rather appeal to you on the basis of love – I, Paul, an old man and now also a prisoner of Christ Jesus – <sup>10</sup>I appeal to you for my child, Onesimus, whose father I became while in chains, <sup>11</sup>who was formerly useless to you, but now is useful to you and to me. <sup>12</sup>I have sent him back to you, that is, my very heart, <sup>13</sup>whom I desired to keep with me, so that on your behalf he might serve me while I am in chains for the gospel, <sup>14</sup>but without your consent I did not want to do anything, so that your goodness might not be out of compulsion, but from your own willingness.

<sup>15</sup>For perhaps it was for this reason he was separated from you for a time, so that you might have him back forever, <sup>16</sup>no longer as a slave, but more than a slave, as a beloved brother (especially to me, but how much more to you), both in the flesh and in the Lord.

<sup>17</sup>So if you consider me your partner, receive him as you would me. <sup>18</sup>But if he has wronged you in any way or owes you anything, charge it to me. <sup>19</sup>I, Paul, have written this with my own hand: I will repay it (not to mention to you that you owe me even your very self). <sup>20</sup>Yes, brother, let me benefit from you in the Lord; refresh my heart in Christ.

<sup>21</sup>Being confident in your obedience I have written to you, knowing that you will do even more than what I say.

<sup>22</sup>And at the same time also, prepare a guest room for me, for I am hoping that through your prayers I will be given to you.

<sup>23</sup>Epaphras, my fellow prisoner in Christ Jesus, greets you, <sup>24</sup>as do Mark, Aristarchus, Demas, and Luke, my fellow workers.

<sup>25</sup>The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit.
