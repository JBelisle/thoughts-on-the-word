## Chapter 13

<sup>1</sup>If I speak in the tongues of men and of angels, but do not have love, I am a noisy gong or a clanging cymbal. <sup>2</sup>And if I have the gift of prophecy, and understand all mysteries and all knowledge, and if I have all faith, so as to remove mountains, but do not have love, I am nothing. <sup>3</sup>And if I give away all my possessions, and if I give over my body, that I might boast, but do not have love, I accomplish nothing.

<sup>4</sup>Love is patient and kind. Love is not envious, does not boast and is not proud. <sup>5</sup>It is not rude, it is not self-serving, it is not easily angered, it keeps no record of wrongs suffered, <sup>6</sup>it does not rejoice in unrighteousness, but rejoices in the truth. <sup>7</sup>It bears all, believes all, hopes all, endures all.

<sup>8</sup>Love never ends, but as for prophecies, they will be brought to an end; as for tongues, they will cease; as for knowledge, it will be brought to an end. <sup>9</sup>For we know in part, and we prophecy in part, <sup>10</sup>but when the perfect[<sup>[a]</sup>](#_ftn1) comes, the partial will be brought to an end. <sup>11</sup>When I was a child, I spoke like a child, I thought like a child, I reasoned like a child; when I became a man, I put an end to childish things. <sup>12</sup>For now we see in a mirror indirectly,[<sup>[b]</sup>](#_ftn2) but then face to face; now I know in part, but then I will know fully just as I also have been fully known.

<sup>13</sup>So now these three remain: faith, hope and love, but the greatest of these is love.

* * *

[<sup>[a]</sup>](#_ftnref1) lit. “complete”

[<sup>[b]</sup>](#_ftnref2) lit. “dimly”
